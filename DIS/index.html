Abstract

Recently, it was shown that small, looped architectures, such as Tiny Recursive Models (TRMs), can outperform Large Language Models (LLMs) on complex reasoning tasks, including the Abstraction and Reasoning Corpus (ARC). In this work, we investigate a core question: how can we further improve the efficiency of these methods with minimal changes?

To address this, we frame the latent reasoning of TRMs as a form of classifier-free guidance and implicit policy improvement algorithm. Building on these insights, we propose a novel training scheme, Deep Improvement Supervision (DIS), that provides a target for each loop during training.

We demonstrate that our approach significantly enhances training efficiency. Our method reduces the total number of forward passes by 18x and eliminates halting mechanisms, while maintaining quality comparable to standard TRMs. Notably, we achieve 24% accuracy on ARC-1 with only 0.8M parameters, outperforming most LLMs.

Methodology

Deep Improvement Supervision (DIS) explicitly enforces a positive Advantage Margin at every recursive step by constructing intermediate targets that contract toward the solution.

Unlike standard TRMs which only supervise the final output, DIS supervises the "reasoning residual" at each step. [cite_start]We effectively treat the latent reasoning process as a discrete diffusion process[cite: 34].

  • 18x Fewer Passes: We avoid the large no-grad cycles used in TRM.
  • No Halting Head: We use a fixed number of improvement steps, simplifying training.

Results

[cite_start]We achieve state-of-the-art performance on complex reasoning benchmarks using a much simpler architecture than the TRM[cite: 41]. [cite_start]With only 0.8M parameters (Compact model), we achieve 24.0% on ARC-1, doubling the performance of the baseline TRM-compact (12.0%)[cite: 412].
